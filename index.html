FAQ

What is the best rep range for muscle growth?

Research shows hypertrophy occurs across a wide range (roughly 5–30 reps) if sets are taken close to failure. The classic 6–12 rep range balances load and time under tension. Renaissance Periodization recommends varying rep ranges within a week — e.g., heavy 5–8, moderate 8–12, light 15–20 — for optimal stimulus.

How many reps for strength vs hypertrophy?

NSCA guidelines: 1–5 reps at 85–100% 1RM for maximal strength; 6–12 reps at 67–85% for hypertrophy. In practice, most programs blend both — strength blocks build the load you can use for hypertrophy sets later.

How long should I rest between sets?

Match rest to the goal: 2–5 minutes for heavy strength (ATP recovery), 60–90 seconds for hypertrophy (metabolic stress), 30–60 seconds for muscular endurance. Short rests with heavy loads compromise performance and increase injury risk.

What is RPE and RIR?

RPE (Rate of Perceived Exertion) scales effort 1–10; RIR (Reps in Reserve) counts reps left in the tank. For strength, stop at RPE 8–9 (1–2 RIR). For hypertrophy, RPE 7–9 (1–3 RIR) on most working sets is standard in RP-style programming.

Can I combine rep ranges in one program?

Yes — periodization is the norm. A typical week might include a heavy 3×5 day (strength), a 4×8–10 day (hypertrophy), and optional 2×15–20 finisher work. Align 70% of volume with your primary goal and use the rest for weak points or variety.
